# PRD Quality Review — KagamiOS

## Overall verdict
This is a faithful, well-disciplined translation of a finalized design spec — the thesis, glossary, Non-Goals, and Success Metrics all trace cleanly to `docs-spec/`, and the FRs are almost uniformly testable rather than aspirational. The two real risks are downstream-facing: two thesis-central spec principles (P5's candidate-comparison shape, E7's human-read requirement) never made it from `docs-spec/` into a testable FR despite the PRD's own claim that every mechanism gets a behavioral-contract FR, and several UJ→FR "realizes" citations point at the wrong FR entirely. Neither is a strategic problem with the PRD's thesis; both are exactly the kind of gap that causes downstream architecture/epics work to quietly diverge from the spec it's supposed to implement.

## Decision-readiness — strong
Trade-offs are named with what's given up, not smoothed to neutral. Non-Goals (§6) each carry a rationale rather than a bare list (e.g. #1: "character increases researcher deference, which the anchoring-discipline principle... exists specifically to prevent"). Open Questions are genuinely open, not rhetorical — OQ-C explicitly states "the spec is explicit that whether they're *sufficient* is fundamentally open, not something this PRD can resolve by building more mechanism" (§9). OQ-A frames a real go/no-go tension ("build MVP now, or run the hand-run validation first and let its outcome adjust MVP scope") without pre-answering it. The single `[NOTE FOR PM]` (§7.2, on the Design Audit Report loop) sits at a real tension — MVP cannot validate its own self-improvement loop — not a safe checkpoint.

### Findings
None — this dimension has no material gaps.

## Substance over theater — strong
No persona theater: only two UJs, explicitly justified inline as illustrations ("per product-shape guidance this section stays light... two journeys, prose beats, no FR-mapping table," §2.3) rather than padding. No NFR theater: §5's five NFRs each name the specific FRs they govern rather than reading as generic boilerplate (e.g. "Determinism before generation, always... governs FR-13, FR-32, FR-37"). Vision (§1) is not swappable — it's anchored in the spec's distinctive "mirror" framing and the specific refusal list ("it will not draft a survey for coverage's sake... treats reaching 'this intuition doesn't survive scrutiny' as fast as possible as a genuine product win"). Non-Goals (§6) read as earned content, each traceable to a specific spec rationale, not template filler.

### Findings
None — this dimension has no material gaps.

## Strategic coherence — strong
The PRD has a clear, faithfully-carried thesis ("a decision, plus the understanding required to make it," §1) and the MVP scope logic follows from it directly rather than from ease: §7.1 states MVP targets "the smallest complete loop the spec identifies, not a subset of features chosen independently by this PRD," pointing to the minimal-run profile (FR-16) rather than a PM-invented cut. Success Metrics (§8) validate the thesis rather than measuring raw activity — SM-1/SM-2/SM-3 are decision-quality and cognitive-ownership signals, not DAU-style counters — and the Counter-metrics (SM-C1–C3) are explicitly load-bearing vetoes per the framing note ("quality/robustness signals are guardrails that veto efficiency changes, never targets to optimize upward on their own"), correctly carrying forward the spec's anti-Goodhart stance from `08_observability.md` §6.

### Findings
None — this dimension has no material gaps.

## Done-ness clarity — adequate
Almost every FR has crisp, testable consequences — no instances of "handles gracefully," "reasonable performance," or similar unbound adjectives were found anywhere in the FR set (grep-verified). This is genuinely strong craft. However, two thesis-central mechanisms from `docs-spec/02_principles.md` never became FRs, despite the PRD's own stated design ("FRs... state the *behavioral contract* each of these mechanisms must satisfy," `addendum.md` A1):

### Findings
- **critical** Missing FR for P5's candidate-comparison shape (§4, Features list; docs-spec `02_principles.md` P5) — `docs-spec` P5 requires Propose to show 3–5 candidates "compared on a fixed qualitative table (same axes for all)... with **no aggregate score**," with the human writing "why this over the others" from the table, not a computed ranking. This never appears as a testable FR — it surfaces only in UJ-1's narrative prose (line 47, "with no aggregate score telling her which to pick"), tagged `(realizes FR-4, FR-10)`, neither of which is actually about this mechanism (FR-4 is constitutive-triad write-guards, FR-10 is draft/accepted lifecycle). The Decide-gate exit criteria in `docs-spec/03_state_machine.md` §3 ("comparison written; per-candidate disposition recorded") are similarly absent from §4's FR set. Without an enforceable FR, downstream architecture has no requirement stopping it from building a ranked/scored candidate list — which is precisely the pattern the spec calls out as anchoring risk ("the human writes... from the table, not from a computed ranking"). Given the explicit instruction not to redesign, a silent gap here is the mechanism by which redesign-by-omission could happen. *Fix:* add an FR under §4.1 or §4.4 (e.g. "Candidate comparison uses a fixed qualitative table with no aggregate score") with consequences covering the table-axes requirement and the "why this over the others" write requirement at Decide.
- **high** Missing FR for E7's human-read requirement (§4; docs-spec `02_principles.md` E7) — E7 states representative papers "carry `human_read` flags with a one-line human reaction, **required at Decide** for the papers defining each surviving candidate." This is the mechanical enforcement of the PRD's own JTBD claim ("I need a decision I can defend to myself," §2.1) and Vision claim ("understanding cannot be delegated," implicit in §1's trace framing) — but no FR in §4 requires it, and the Glossary's Cluster Dossier entry (§3) mentions "representative papers" without the read/reaction gate. *Fix:* add an FR (likely under §4.1 or §4.2) making the Decide gate's exit criteria explicit, including the `human_read` + reaction requirement for representative papers behind surviving candidates.
- **medium** Decide gate's own exit criteria are nowhere codified as an FR — beyond the provisional-count (FR-20) and unprimed-diff (FR-24) pieces, `docs-spec/03_state_machine.md` §3's full Decide exit criteria ("checklist trace-complete; comparison written; per-candidate disposition recorded; decision signed") has no corresponding FR. This is the same gap as the P5 finding above, but calling it out separately since it's the natural place an engineer would look for "what does 'done' mean for the Decide gate" and would currently find nothing.

## Scope honesty — strong
Non-Goals (§6) do real work, each with a specific mechanism-level rationale and most citing the originating docs-spec rejection. `[ASSUMPTION]` tags mark genuine PM-framing calls (§1's vision condensation, §2.3's invented UJ personas) rather than hiding them. Open-items density (6 Open Questions, 5 Assumptions, 1 NOTE FOR PM) is proportionate to the stated stakes tier (Internal tool, ~5–8pg, Fast path per `.memlog.md`) and, more importantly, proportionate to the fact that several of these are the *spec's own* unresolved open items (OQ-B through OQ-F map 1:1 to `docs-spec/09_open_items.md` OQ2–OQ5, OQ7) rather than PM-introduced uncertainty — this PRD is not smoothing over spec-level open questions, it's carrying them forward honestly.

### Findings
None — this dimension has no material gaps.

## Downstream usability — adequate
Glossary (§3) is disciplined — spot-checked terms (Direction Decision, Cluster Dossier, Confidence Checklist, Skeptic) are used identically everywhere they appear, with no synonym drift found (e.g. `docs-spec` sometimes says "Decision memo," but the PRD consistently normalizes to "Direction Decision" per its own Glossary — correct behavior, not drift). FR IDs (FR-1–FR-41) are contiguous and unique. Section 9's Open Questions map cleanly to `docs-spec` OQ1–OQ7 (OQ6 correctly absorbed into §2.2 Non-Users rather than restated). However, the UJ→FR "realizes" cross-references — exactly the kind of annotation a downstream story-writer would use to jump from a UJ beat to its enforcing FR — contain real errors:

### Findings
- **high** Broken UJ→FR cross-references in §2.3 (Key User Journeys) — UJ-1's clustering beat ("offers her two structurally different ways to cluster the field... `(realizes FR-3, FR-25)`", line 46) cites FR-3 (per-cluster derived state) and FR-25 (Scout is the sole corpus-touching role), neither of which concerns clustering. The FR that actually governs this — FR-26 ("Cartographer must offer at least two structurally different cuts") — is instead cited in UJ-2's *Skeptic* beat (line 54, "The Skeptic role flags this directly... `(realizes FR-26)`"), where the correct citation should be FR-27 ("Skeptic attacks only, with a fresh context"). This reads as an off-by-one citation shift between the two UJs. *Fix:* swap the citations — UJ-1's clustering beat should cite FR-26, UJ-2's Skeptic beat should cite FR-27.
- **low** Assumptions Index roundtrip incomplete (§10) — of the 5 index entries, only 2 (§1, §2.3) have a corresponding inline `[ASSUMPTION: ...]` tag in the body (grep-verified). The other three — §7.1's MVP-scope interpretation, "Throughout §4"'s FR-grouping choice, and the stakes-tier/working-mode default — are index-only, with no inline marker at their point of occurrence. This is a minor roundtrip break relative to the rubric's expectation that every inline tag is indexed *and* every index entry is inline-tagged; the §7.1 and stakes-tier entries are arguably fine to leave index-only (they're process/calibration notes, not textual claims), but "Throughout §4" in particular describes a structural choice a reader skimming §4 would have no inline signal about.

## Shape fit — strong
The PRD correctly resists over-formalization for this shape. §2.3 explicitly names the product-shape reasoning for keeping UJs light ("KagamiOS has a single user role (the researcher) interacting with a system of internal AI role-contracts, not multiple human personas... this section stays light: two journeys, prose beats, no FR-mapping table") rather than defaulting to a persona template. The AI roles (Interviewer, Scout, Cartographer, Historian, Skeptic) are correctly treated throughout as behavioral contracts enforced by write-guards and generation windows (§4.4), never as personas or characters — reinforced by Non-Goal #1 explicitly forbidding anthropomorphization. §2.2's Non-Users section does real shape-defining work (advisor-as-consumer-not-gate) that a persona table would not have surfaced as cleanly.

### Findings
None — this dimension has no material gaps.
